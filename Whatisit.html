What is it?

  • My Story
    •  Cranial Fronto Nasal Dysplaysia is a genetic condition. It mainly affects the physical appearance of the head and face but can affect different parts on the body. The skull is either fully or partially fused together before birth. The eyes tend to be widely spaced and there may be a bifid (split) nose. males with this condition may only have widely spaced eyes and no other symptom.
    There is a 25% chance that the child will have CFND. There is a 50% chance that the child will be a carrier.
    In the table below I have listed the most common symptoms of the condtion there are ofcourse more.

    Medical names Meaning Outcome
    Depressed nasal ridge Flat nose operation to make a bridge
    Hypertelorism Wide-Set eyes major operation to reset facial bones
    Camptodactyly of finger Permanent flexion of the finger Can be left alone or an operation to permanently straighten it
    Cleft Palate Cleft roof of mouth No issues
    Extra bones Extra bones in shoulders Major surgery to remove bones, requires a scar down your spine
    fused skull born with a fused skull Operation to relieve pressure so the brain can grow, usually in the first few months of birth

    Cranial Fronto Nasal Dysplasia is treated by a multidisciplinary team, they will ususally comprise of Craniofacial/Maxillofacial (skull and face) surgeons, Neuroscientist (brain), Opthalmologists (eye specialists) Audiologists (ear specialists) and Geneticists. the inital skull re-shaping surgery takes place within the first year of life.
